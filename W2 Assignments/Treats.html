Pumpkin Pie Cheesecake Truffles
Full Recipe and Preperation
Craving something small, sweet, and easy to make? This treat is yummy and fun for the family!

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 14oz sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup white chocolate chips
  • Orange food coloring
  • Granulated sugar, as needed
  • Chocolate chips, for topping

Preparation

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine butter, cream cheese, pumpkin puree, sweetened condensed milk, and pumpkin pie spice over medium heat. Stir until well combined and thickened.
  2. Add white chocolate and graham cracker crumbs and stir until white chocolate has melted.
  3. Add food coloring until desired color is reached. Continue stirring until pumpkin mixture has thickened and releases from the sides of the pan.
  4. Transfer mixture to a greased baking sheet and spread out with a rubber spatula. Chill until firm, at least 2 hours and up to overnight.
  5. Once chilled, coat your hands in butter, then roll chilled dough into small balls.
  6. Fill a shallow bowl with sugar, then roll balls in sugar to coat.
  7. Using a toothpick, make ridges on the truffle so it resembles a pumpkin and top with a chocolate chip.
  8. Chill until ready to serve. Enjoy!

Spooky Black Caramel Apples
Full Recipe and Preperation
These apples are sure to rule the party! (We promise they're not poisonous)

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp corn syrup
  • 2 Tbsp water
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2-3 tsp black food coloring
  • 2 Tbsp unsalted butter

Preparation

  1. Line a baking sheet with a silicone mat or parchment paper.
  2. Prepare apples: wash, dry, and remove stems. Insert sticks or popsicle/craft sticks. Set aside.
  3. In a meidum heat proof bowl, add corn syrup, water, and granulated sugar. Microwave for 5-7 minutes until mixture boils and turns a light amber color. Watch closely and remove immediately after it has reached ideal shade.
  4. Stir in heavy cream, followed by salt, vanilla, and food coloring. Mix until well incorporated. Finally stir in butter until smooth.
  5. Immediately dip and coat one apple at a time. Tilt and rotate to coat completely. Hold over bowl with stick up, allowing excess to drip off or 10-15 seconds. flip upsdie down, with stick down, letting caramel set for 15-20 seconds. Transfer to prepared baking sheet. Repeat with remaining apples.
  6. Chill for at least 30-60 mintes to completely set caramel.
  7. Can be made up to a day ahead.

Halloween Chocolate-Covered Strawberries
Full Recipe and Preperation
Don't like pushing daisies? Push strawberries instead!

Ingredients

  • 6 ounces dark-green, white, and/or orange candy melting wafers
  • 1-2 tsp coconut oil or vegetable shortening
  • 1/2 cup dark-green sanding sugar (for monsters)
  • 12 large strawberries
  • 24 small candy eyeballs (for monsters)
  • 1 ounce chocolate chips and/or black candy melting wafers for decoration

Preparation

  1. Melt your choice of candy wafers in a bowl. Stir. Add coconut oil as needed if wafers start to harden.
  2. Dip strawberries into melted wafers. For the monster, after this step, coat the strawberries in the dark-green sugar.
  3. Cool in refrigerator for 10 minutes
  4. Use 1 ounce chocolate chips or black candy melting wafers as "glue" for eyes or for other decoration.
  5. Cool in refrigerator again for 10-15 minutes before eating.
  6. Enjoy!
